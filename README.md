1. Project Title

E-Commerce Sales Dashboard – Power BI

An interactive Power BI dashboard designed to analyze e-commerce sales performance across states, product categories, payment modes, and time periods.

2. Short Description / Purpose

This dashboard helps understand how an e-commerce business is performing by tracking sales, profit, quantity sold, and customer trends. It exists to support data-driven decisions by giving a clear view of what’s selling, where revenue is coming from, and which areas need improvement.

3. Tech Stack

1.Power BI Desktop – Data modeling & visualization

2.DAX (Data Analysis Expressions) – Measures and KPIs

3.Power Query – Data cleaning and transformation

4.Microsoft Excel / CSV – Source data

4. Features / Highlights


Business Problem

1.E-commerce businesses often struggle to understand:

2.Which states and categories generate the most revenue

3.How profit changes month over month

4.Which payment methods customers prefer

5.Who the top customers are

Without a centralized dashboard, these insights remain hidden in raw data.


Goal of the Dashboard

The main goal was to create a single-page interactive dashboard that:

1.Tracks overall sales and profitability

2.Identifies high-performing states and product categories

3.Analyzes customer behavior and payment preferences

4.Helps business users quickly spot trends and patterns

Walkthrough of Key Visuals

KPI Cards (Top Section)
Displays key metrics like:

1.Total Sales Amount (161K)

2.Total Profit (26K)

3.Total Quantity Sold (2008)

4.Average Order Value (44K)
These KPIs give a quick snapshot of overall business health.

5.Sales by State (Bar Chart)
Shows state-wise revenue contribution, helping identify top-performing regions like Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh.

6.Quantity by Category (Donut Chart)
Highlights product category performance, where Clothing contributes the highest share, followed by Electronics and Furniture.

7.Profit by Month (Column Chart)
Tracks monthly profit trends, making it easy to spot peak and low-performing months.

8.Sales by Customer (Bar Chart)
Identifies top customers based on total purchase value, useful for loyalty and retention strategies.

9.Quantity by Payment Mode (Donut Chart)
Shows customer payment preferences, with Cash on Delivery (COD) being the most used mode.

10.Filters & Slicers

11.Quarter-wise filter (Q1–Q4)

12.State-level filter
These allow users to interactively explore data from different perspectives.

Business Impact & Insights

1.Clothing is the highest-selling category, indicating strong demand

2.COD dominates payment modes, suggesting trust or accessibility factors

3.Certain states consistently outperform others in revenue contribution

4.Monthly profit trends help plan inventory and marketing campaigns


Overall, the dashboard enables faster decision-making, better sales analysis, and improved business visibility.
